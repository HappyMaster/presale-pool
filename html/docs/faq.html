is this product safe and secure?

We do not ask you to provide your wallet credentials in order to transact with the presale contract. Instead, we will provide you with the data you need to perform the transaction yourself on MEW or some other ethereum wallet platform. Our code is also open source and you can view it on our Github. However, while your ether remains in the pool, it is in the control of the pool manager. Make sure to only contribute to pools managed by someone you trust.

How do I know EtherPool can’t see my private key?

We’ve opted not to ask for your wallet credentials. Instead, we will generate the data for you to perform the transactions yourself on MyEtherWallet.

is there a cap on how much i can raise?

We have no caps.

Is there a limit to the number of investors?

There is no limit to the number of members you can have in your pool. However, there are costs associated with adding a whitelist to the pool which can effectively limit the size of your pool (see answer below).

what are the transaction costs?

Every interaction you have with the contract, either as the pool manager or as a contributor, will cost gas. Most operations cost a fixed amount of gas. However, adding a whitelist has a cost proportional to the number of participants in the pool. And, in some cases, editing contribution limits can have a gas cost proportional to how many participants are currently in the pool. Modifying an existing whitelist has a cost proportional to the number of additions/deletions you want to make to the whitelist. Due to gas limits, we recommend including a maximum of 50 members at a time when modifying whitelists. So if you would like to have a whitelist of 150 members, we recommend splitting that into 3 separate ethereum transactions each consisting of 50 members.

Can i withdraw my investment?

While the pool retains its open status, you are free to adjust your contribution size. But once a pool has a closed status, you will not be able to make adjustments to your contribution. For any number of reasons, the pool manager may decide to put the pool into a failed state, at which point you’ll be able to withdraw all your contribution.

how do i get my tokens?

You will be able to retrieve your tokens from the same EtherPool address where you invested ether. The ICO will send them to that address and you will be able to withdraw them by executing a transaction. As for timing, a good indicator will be the ICO roadmap that the particular ICO published on its website. Your pool creator will likely have more details based on negotiations s/he made with the ICO team. In v2, we plan to personalize the product much more. One way will be to display total token availability and your share.
